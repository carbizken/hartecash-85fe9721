## THE 30-SECOND PITCH

Every franchise and independent dealer in America needs more used cars. The auction lanes are thin, the prices are ugly, and the only reliable way to feed a used-car department is to buy directly from consumers and off the service drive. The tools the industry uses to do that today — **KBB Instant Cash Offer, AutoHub/AccuTrade, TrueCar Trade-In, Edmunds, and the Cox ecosystem** — were built a decade ago as lead-gen funnels for listing sites. They are expensive, generic, slow to change, and they do not care whether the dealer actually buys the car.

**Autocurb.io is the first vehicle-acquisition platform built from the customer click to the vAuto push — as one continuous system.** We turn a dealership's website, service drive, BDC, sales floor, and used-car manager into a single acquisition engine, priced at **$1,495–$1,995 per rooftop per month** — less than what a single dealership pays KBB ICO for a feed of recycled leads.

---

## THE PROBLEM WE SOLVE

Used-car gross is the single largest profit center left in franchise retail, and the hardest problem in retail is **sourcing inventory at a cost that still allows a profit**. Dealers have five broken options today:

1. **Auctions (Manheim / ADESA)** — Prices are at or above retail. Fees, transport, recon, and lane risk eat the margin before the car hits the lot.
2. **Trade-ins** — Only as good as the walk-in traffic. Half the trades the dealer wants to keep are upside-down or get "shopped" against another store.
3. **KBB Instant Cash Offer** — A glorified lead form. The dealer pays ~$1,500–$2,500/mo, gets a price the customer has already shopped on three other sites, and still has to do all the work to close the car.
4. **AutoHub / AccuTrade (Cox)** — Bundles appraisal + data, but it's a tool inside vAuto, not a consumer acquisition funnel. Customers never touch it directly. The dealer pays thousands for what is essentially an appraisal calculator.
5. **Service-drive programs (SellUsYourCar, Carfax History-Based Value, etc.)** — Dependent on a service advisor remembering to push a button. Conversion is abysmal.

**Every one of these tools stops at the offer.** Nobody ties the website form to the BDC, to the inspection, to the appraisal, to the ACV, to the check request, to the title work, and finally to the push into vAuto inventory. That gap is where dealers lose 60–80% of the cars they "offered."

Autocurb.io closes that gap end-to-end.

## THE GROWTH PATH — FROM ACQUISITION TO vAUTO PUSH

This is where the platform stops being a CRM and becomes infrastructure.

**Phase 1 — Acquisition (shipping today):** Customer → offer → BDC → inspection → ACV → check request → paid.

**Phase 2 — Backgrounding (next build):** As soon as the check is cut, the car auto-enrolls in our **backgrounding workflow**: photo capture in the service lane, reconditioning checklist, mechanical inspection sync, title work tracking, frontline readiness scoring. The used car manager can see every acquired unit's recon status on a single board.

**Phase 3 — vAuto push:** When the unit clears recon, Autocurb pushes the full vehicle record — VIN, condition data, damage notes, photos, appraisal, ACV, recon costs, and frontline date — **directly into vAuto** via their dealer-authorized API. The dealer never hand-types a car into inventory again. We become the **system of record** for used inventory *before* it hits vAuto, which makes us the data source that vAuto depends on — not the other way around.

Once that pipeline is live, **Autocurb owns the dealer's entire used-car supply chain**, from the consumer's first click to the frontline price tag. That is a vAuto-scale business hiding inside a KBB-scale price point.
